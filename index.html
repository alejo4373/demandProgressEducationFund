Demand Progress Education Fund


About Us

Demand Progress Education Fund is a nonprofit 501(c)(3) entity sponsored by the New Venture Fund.

-We educate our more than two million members and the general public about matters pertaining to the democratic nature of our nation’s communications infrastructure and governance structures, and the impacts of corporate power over our economy and democracy.

-We mobilize our members and the public to engage with executive branch agencies with jurisdiction over these matters, and to confront corporate bad actors.

-We engage in research, outreach to the media, educational programing, and occasional litigation related to these areas of concern.


Areas of Focus

Internet Freedom

We work to protect an internet that affords the public the ability to communicate freely online, without unjust interference from corporations or governments.

Human and Civil Rights

We strive to protect human and civil rights, online and off, in the United States and throughout the world.

Corporate Power

We work to eliminate the corrupting influence of big money in politics and reform the financial system in order to ensure our democracy is for everyone, not a tool for wealthy interests to rig the game.

Democracy and Open Government

An open and accountable government is essential for a well-functioning democracy — and smart use of technology is key to making our modern democracy work.

Some of our recent and ongoing efforts are outlined below:

Taking on corporate power

We fight to ensure that officials nominate public interest-minded people to oversee corporate regulatory agencies
We oppose the Sprint, T-Mobile merger, and encourage the FCC to block it
We co-chair the Freedom From Facebook coalition , and encourage authorities to break up Facebook
We encourage the FTC to crack down on those who collude to suppress wages
We are urging the FTC to ban noncompete agreements
We are encouraging the FTC to block Staples' acquisition of Essendant
We are calling on Congress to use its oversight powers, specifically to examine the merger between BB&T and SunTrust banks

Protecting human and civil rights

We sit at the Leadership Conference/Ford Foundation Civil Rights, Technology, and Privacy Table
We organize the 4th Amendment Advisory Committee and advise on security best practices, while regularly hosting trainings and educational events about the impacts of mass surveillance

Enhancing democracy and making government more open

We co-lead the Progressive Talent Pipeline , through which we encourage progressives to take jobs in government and facilitate their professional development
We publish the weekly First Branch Forecast newsletter
We co-organize the Congressional Data Coalition

We regularly write longform reports, including on matters such as:

  • The House Inspector General
  • The US Capitol Police
  • Daycare availability for Hill staff
  • House committee funding
